Fake News

Fake news is a modern tool that can help influence a group of people to a large extent. But how effective is this tool really? Does it only work on older people who read news on their computer? Do people really think critically anymore or do they blindly believe what they read, see and hear? These are some of the questions we thought we would visualize for you with a small round of interviews with a few different people who had no idea what we were doing.

Fake news test

To show how difficult it can actually be to distinguish between fake news and not fake news based on just a screenshot, like just hearing it on the street. here you can look through the picture, go to the link if you wish and see if you can guess/find out whether it is fake or true news!
If you press the button below the text boxes, the summary will appear.
if you click on the pictures you will get to the websites!

A tweet from one of the world's most influential rich people, Emmanuel Macron (The President of France as of 2022). Here Macron writes about the rainforest and specifically how much oxygen it produces for life on Earth.

Fake news!

What Macron writes here regarding the rainforest contributing to 20% of Earth's oxygen has been scientifically disproven. although Macron may not have known this was not true he helps that such statements are gently trusted by others.

In this article published on Foxnews, it is written about how the United States Bacon reservoir is at the lowest it has been in 50 years. here they have figures like: there are only 17.8 million pounds of pork belly they can convert to bacon and that it is the lowest since 1957.

Fake news!

Amazingly, this is also fake news even though this was published by Fox news which is actually a source many people blindly follow and see as believable.

in this article published by National geographic they write about how it is not possible for the rainforest to be able to produce 20% of the earth's oxygen. Here they write about other researchers who believe it is less than 1%

true news!

the fact that the rainforest can produce less than 1% of the earth's oxygen is actually true! The article does not try to disprove that we should stop to cutting in the rainforest but it only points out that it is wrong for politicians and others to use misinformation as facts to reinforce a meaning.
